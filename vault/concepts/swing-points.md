## What is a Swing Point?

A **swing high** is a candle whose high has a **lower high on both sides** — the middle candle is the swing high. A **swing low** is the mirror: a low with a **higher low on each side**. These are the precise pivots that define [market structure](market-structure.md) and mark [liquidity](liquidity.md) pools.

```
   Swing High:              Swing Low:
        ╱╲                   ──╲    ╱──
   ────╱  ╲────                 ╲  ╱
   L    H    L                   ╲╱
   (lower highs               H    L    H
    either side)              (higher lows either side)
```

---

## Nesting: Short / Intermediate / Long-Term

Swing points **nest** — a bigger swing is built from smaller ones. ICT classifies three degrees:

| Degree | Notation (halos) | Meaning |
|--------|------------------|---------|
| **Short-term high/low (STH/STL)** | ○ one halo | A basic swing point |
| **Intermediate-term high/low (ITH/ITL)** | ◎ double halo | A swing with STHs on both sides |
| **Long-term high/low (LTH/LTL)** | ◉ triple halo | A swing with ITHs on both sides |

> "It's not just lower-low, lower-high — you've got to look at the **nesting effect** of the highs and lows."

---

## Why Nesting Matters

- Tells you the **degree** of a level — an intermediate/long-term high is far more significant than a short-term one
- A break of a **higher-degree** swing is a stronger [market structure shift](market-structure-shift.md)
- Entering near an **intermediate-term high** (with structure confirming bearish) gives an [optimal entry](optimal-trade-entry.md) with tight risk
- Reading structure by degree beats the naive "lower high / lower low" read

---

## How to Use It

1. Mark every swing high/low (lower-high-both-sides / higher-low-both-sides)
2. **Classify** each: short / intermediate / long-term (halo count)
3. Read the trend by the **degree** of swings being broken
4. Target and enter around **higher-degree** swings (they hold more [liquidity](liquidity.md))
