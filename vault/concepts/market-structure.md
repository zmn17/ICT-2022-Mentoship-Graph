## Overview

Market structure in the ICT framework uses the daily chart range (swing high to swing low) and the **50% Fibonacci retracement** to divide price into discount and premium zones. This determines directional bias and identifies high-probability areas for entries.

---

## Discount vs. Premium

The current trading range is divided at the **50% Fibonacci retracement**:

```
Swing High ───────────────────── 100%
         │
         │   PREMIUM ZONE        (above 50%)
         │   → Look for SELLS
         │
50% Fib  ─────────────────────── Equilibrium
         │
         │   DISCOUNT ZONE       (below 50%)
         │   → Look for BUYS
         │
Swing Low ────────────────────── 0%
```

### Discount Zone (Below 50%)
- Price is trading at a "bargain" relative to the range
- Smart money is a **buyer** in discount
- Look for bullish [order blocks](order-blocks.md) and [fair value gaps](fair-value-gaps.md)
- This is where accumulation occurs before the distribution rally

### Premium Zone (Above 50%)
- Price is trading at a "markup" relative to the range
- Smart money is a **seller** in premium
- Look for bearish order blocks and FVGs
- This is where distribution occurs before the sell-off

---

## Fibonacci Application (From Episode 9)

The presenter uses the **daily chart swing** to draw Fibonacci levels:

1. Identify the most recent significant **swing high** and **swing low** on the daily chart
2. Draw Fibonacci from high to low
3. The **50% level** (0.5 retracement) is the equilibrium line
4. Trading below this level = discount; above = premium

### Key Observation:
> "Mondays typically show price dropping into discount, testing sell-side liquidity below short-term lows, followed by attempts to retrace."

This means Monday price action often pushes into discount to complete the manipulation phase before rallying.

---

## How Market Structure Guides Trading

### Bullish Bias (Discount)
When price is **below the 50% Fib**:
- Expect [sell-side liquidity](liquidity.md) hunts (price dips to take stops)
- Look for [bullish order blocks](order-blocks.md) to buy into
- Target: Price returning to equilibrium or premium (above 50%)
- This aligns with the accumulation → distribution flow of [Power of 3](power-of-3.md)

### Bearish Bias (Premium)
When price is **above the 50% Fib**:
- Expect [buy-side liquidity](liquidity.md) hunts (price spikes to take stops)
- Look for bearish order blocks to sell into
- Target: Price dropping to equilibrium or discount (below 50%)

## Daily Chart Range and Intraday Context

From Episode 9, the daily chart provides the **framework**:

1. **Identify the range:** Mark the daily swing high and low
2. **Draw the 50%:** This is your bias line
3. **Determine where Monday opens:** If price opens in discount, the weekly bias is bullish
4. **Intraday application:** Use 5-minute chart to find entries aligned with the daily bias

---

## Market Structure and Liquidity Hunts

When price is in discount and hunts sell-side liquidity:
- This is a **high-probability** reversal zone
- [Order blocks](order-blocks.md) in discount + liquidity sweep = strong buy signal
- The [Power of 3](power-of-3.md) manipulation is complete
- Distribution (the rally) should follow

When price is in premium and hunts buy-side liquidity:
- This is a **high-probability** reversal zone for shorts
- Bearish OBs in premium + liquidity sweep = strong sell signal

## Confluence Hierarchy

The highest-probability setups from Episode 9 stack these factors:

1. ✅ Price in **discount zone** (below 50% Fib)
2. ✅ [Sell-side liquidity](liquidity.md) has been **swept**
3. ✅ Price is at a [bullish order block](order-blocks.md)
4. ✅ A [fair value gap](fair-value-gaps.md) overlaps the OB zone
5. ✅ It's the [NY PM session](../episodes/ep09-power-of-3-ny-pm/new-york-pm-session.md) (1:00–3:00 PM)

All five = maximum probability. Three or more = tradeable.
