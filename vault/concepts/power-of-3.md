## What is Power of 3?

Power of 3 is ICT's framework describing the three phases that institutional/algorithmic price action cycles through:

```
┌─────────────────┐     ┌─────────────────┐     ┌─────────────────┐
│  ACCUMULATION   │ ──► │  MANIPULATION   │ ──► │  DISTRIBUTION   │
│  (Consolidation)│     │  (Fake-out/Hunt)│     │  (True Move)    │
└─────────────────┘     └─────────────────┘     └─────────────────┘
```

---

## Phase 1: Accumulation

- Price consolidates in a tight range
- Smart money quietly builds positions
- Occurs during low-volume periods (e.g., Asia session, pre-market)
- Appears as sideways/range-bound price action on charts
- Often visible as Monday's price action setting up the week's range

**What to look for:**
- Tight daily ranges
- Consolidation near key levels
- Low volatility periods preceding session opens

---

## Phase 2: Manipulation

- Price breaks out of the accumulation range in a **false direction**
- Designed to trigger retail stop-losses and induce breakout traders
- Creates [liquidity](liquidity.md) for smart money to fill orders against
- Often appears as a "Judas swing" — a deceptive move that traps traders

**What to look for:**
- Price sweeping above/below short-term highs/lows
- [Sell-side liquidity](liquidity.md) being taken below relative equal lows
- Quick, aggressive moves that reverse sharply
- Moves into [order blocks](order-blocks.md) that then hold

---

## Phase 3: Distribution

- The **true directional move** after manipulation is complete
- Smart money distributes (sells) positions acquired during accumulation
- Moves toward the actual target — often the opposite side's liquidity
- Creates [fair value gaps](fair-value-gaps.md) as price displaces aggressively

**What to look for:**
- Strong displacement candles after manipulation completes
- Price targeting the opposite liquidity pool
- Clean breaks of structure with FVGs left behind

---

## Power of 3 Applied to Daily Chart

From Episode 9's analysis of NASDAQ E-mini futures:

1. **Monday's accumulation:** Price drops into discount (below 50% Fibonacci), testing sell-side liquidity below short-term lows
2. **Manipulation:** Liquidity hunt below Monday's lows, taking out retail stops
3. **Distribution:** Price rallies through the week, targeting buy-side liquidity above

---

## Power of 3 Applied Intraday

The same three phases repeat on lower time frames within a single session:

1. **Pre-market accumulation:** London/overnight session establishes range
2. **NY open manipulation:** Price dips to take out overnight lows (or rallies to take highs)
3. **NY PM distribution:** True directional move occurs after 1:00 PM when lunch manipulation is complete

See [New York PM Session](../episodes/ep09-power-of-3-ny-pm/new-york-pm-session.md) for detailed intraday timing.

---

## Key Insight from Episode 9

> "Power 3 structure enables traders to anticipate fake-outs and liquidity hunts, providing better entry points."

The framework allows you to:
- **Not chase** the manipulation move (avoid being trapped)
- **Wait patiently** for the manipulation to complete
- **Enter** during distribution with high confidence and tight stops

## Power Three — the Daily Candle (Ep 10)

Episode 10 applies Power of 3 to the shape of a **single daily candle** (open → manipulate → distribute):

| Day type | Candle shape |
|----------|--------------|
| **Bullish** | Opens near the **low**, closes near the **high** (accumulate at open, expand up) |
| **Bearish** | Opens near the **high** (rallies slightly), closes near the **low** (distribute down) |

> Implication: **hold toward the candle close** to capture the full daily expansion. This is the basis for reading [daily bias](daily-bias.md).
