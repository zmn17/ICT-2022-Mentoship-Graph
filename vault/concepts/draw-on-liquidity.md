## What is a Draw on Liquidity?

A "draw on liquidity" is the **liquidity pool that price is being pulled toward** — it acts like a **magnet**, defining the directional bias for the session or trade. Once you identify where the untapped [liquidity](liquidity.md) rests, seemingly random fluctuations become **purposeful**: price is working its way toward that magnet.

---

## The Magnet Model

```
   price chops / consolidates / fakes  ← looks like noise…
              │
              ▼  …but is really being DRAWN toward:
   ═══════════════════  resting sell-side liquidity (old low)
                        = the draw / the magnet / the objective
```

> An untapped old low that "hasn't traded down there yet" acts like a **big magnet** — the fluctuations that look like noise are actually price being drawn to it.

---

## How It Sets Bias

1. Find the **untapped liquidity** — sell-side below an old low, buy-side above an old high
2. That pool is the **draw** — the target price is destined to attack
3. Bias = **toward the draw**: draw below → bearish; draw above → bullish
4. Retracements *away* from the draw are **traps** (e.g., a [Judas swing](judas-swing.md)) — opportunities to enter *toward* the draw

---

## Why It Matters

- Turns "where is price going?" into "where is the **unfilled liquidity**?"
- Explains why consolidations resolve in a specific direction
- A retracement into premium when the draw is **below** = a **selling** opportunity, not a genuine reversal
- Pairs with [premium/discount](market-structure.md): sell premium when the draw is a discount low
