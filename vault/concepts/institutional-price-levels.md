## What Are Institutional Price Levels?

Institutional (or "big figure") price levels are the **round numbers** algorithms reference: the **00, 20, 50, and 80** handles of each big figure. Price gravitates to and reacts at these levels because [liquidity](liquidity.md) and orders cluster there. In FX, the "big figure" is the round pip level (e.g., **1.09** = the "1.09 big figure").

---

## The Quarters

```
   ...00 ──────────  full big figure (e.g., 1.0900)
   ...80 ──────────
   ...50 ──────────  half
   ...20 ──────────
   ...00 ──────────  next big figure down (e.g., 1.0800)
```

The 00 / 20 / 50 / 80 handles quarter each 100-pip (or 100-point) range. These are natural **magnets** and **reaction points**.

---

## Why They Matter

- Retail orders and stops cluster at **round numbers** → [liquidity](liquidity.md) pools
- Algorithms **measure to** these levels — targets and reversals often land on them
- A big figure like **1.09** becomes a **magnet** where liquidity + [Fibonacci projections](fibonacci-targeting.md) **converge**
- Confluence of a big figure **and** relative equal lows **and** a std-dev projection = a high-confidence target

---

## How to Use Them

1. Mark the **00/20/50/80** levels around current price
2. Look for a big figure where **multiple factors converge** (equal highs/lows, [Fib projection](fibonacci-targeting.md), [order block](order-blocks.md))
3. Use it as a **target** (draw) or a **reaction** level for entries
4. Expect stops/liquidity to rest just beyond the round number
