## What is Displacement?

Displacement is a **pronounced, energetic break** above a prior swing high or below a prior swing low. It is not a mild drift — it's an aggressive, one-directional move that signals a **stop hunt / liquidity grab** and the intent of smart money. Displacement is what leaves behind a [Fair Value Gap](fair-value-gaps.md).

---

## Displacement vs. a Normal Move

| | Normal move | Displacement |
|---|---|---|
| Energy | Gradual, overlapping candles | Sharp, forceful, large-bodied candles |
| Meaning | Noise / drift | Liquidity grab + directional intent |
| Leaves an FVG? | Rarely | **Yes** — this is the key signature |
| Tradeable? | Low signal | High-probability setup |

> Example: price breaks above an old high, then **sharply reverses with strength** — not a mild retracement. That energy is displacement.

---

## Why Displacement Matters

- It confirms a [stop hunt](stop-hunts.md) actually occurred (liquidity was taken with force)
- The velocity leaves an **imbalance** — a [Fair Value Gap](fair-value-gaps.md) — in its wake
- That FVG becomes your **entry zone** on the retracement
- It marks the transition from liquidity grab to the real directional move

---

## The Displacement → FVG → Entry Sequence

```
1. DISPLACE  → Price energetically breaks a swing high/low
              (e.g., runs buy stops above an old high)
                        │
              The forceful move leaves a Fair Value Gap
                        │
2. RETRACE   → Price pulls back INTO the FVG
                        │
3. ENTER     → Trade in the direction of the displacement's
              *resolution* (e.g., short after a buy-stop run
              that reverses down)
```

This is the actionable core: **wait for the FVG left by displacement, then enter on the retrace** — don't chase the displacement candle itself. See [Fair Value Gaps](fair-value-gaps.md).

---

## Displacement in Both Directions

- **Bearish displacement:** energetic break *below* a swing low → leaves a bearish FVG above → sell the retrace
- **Bullish displacement:** energetic break *above* a swing high (or a sharp reversal up after a sell-stop run) → leaves a bullish FVG below → buy the retrace

Often the most powerful setups are a displacement that **reverses** a liquidity grab: price runs stops one way, then displaces hard the other way.

---

## Where It Fits in the Setup

Displacement is the bridge between liquidity being taken and the entry:

```
Swing high/low (liquidity)  →  STOP HUNT  →  DISPLACEMENT  →  FVG  →  Entry
```

- [Three Drives](three-drives.md) or a swing point identifies the liquidity
- [Stop Hunts](stop-hunts.md) take that liquidity
- **Displacement** is the energetic move that confirms it and leaves the FVG
- [Fair Value Gaps](fair-value-gaps.md) give the entry
