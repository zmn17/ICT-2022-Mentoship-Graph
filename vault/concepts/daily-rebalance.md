## What is Daily Rebalance Theory?

A **large-range daily move** (a big distribution or accumulation day) leaves an **inefficiency** in the daily range. The **next day** price naturally **retraces to rebalance** that inefficiency — usually back to a **logical level** like the prior day's low/high — **before resuming** in the direction of the underlying [bias](daily-bias.md).

---

## The Key Trap

> When Tuesday rallies back up and **rebalances Monday's big drop**, it **tricks traders** into thinking "the low is in, it's going higher." In reality price has only gone up to a **logical level** (e.g., Friday's low) that **rebalances the selloff** — a **premium** — before continuing **lower**.

```
   Friday low ─────────  ← logical rebalance level (a premium after the drop)
        ▲ Tuesday rallies UP to here = rebalances Monday's range
        │  (looks bullish — it's a Judas swing to premium)
   ─────┼──────  Monday: large-range DISTRIBUTION day (the inefficiency)
        ▼ then resume DOWN (bias) → attack sell-side discount
```

---

## Judge the Imbalance on the Right Timeframe

> On the **15-minute** the rally "does not look like an imbalance" — but on the **daily candle** Monday was a large down day, so all that movement is **distribution** leaving an inefficiency.

- An imbalance must be judged on **the timeframe that produced the range**
- A big **daily** move creates a **daily** inefficiency, even if lower timeframes look clean

---

## How It Fits the Model

Daily Rebalance is the **retracement half** of the day, tied to [Power of 3](power-of-3.md):

```
8:30 open = accumulation
rally to rebalance the prior big day = manipulation (Judas swing to premium)
drop from the rebalance level = distribution (toward discount sell-side)
```

- Once price hits the rebalance level, look for the entry pattern: **FVG + market structure shift + displacement** down
- Target the **discount array** ([sell-side liquidity](liquidity.md) below old lows)

---

## How to Use It

1. Note a **large-range daily** move (distribution or accumulation)
2. Expect the next day to **retrace to rebalance** it — to a **logical level** (prior day's low/high)
3. Don't be tricked — that rally into premium is a **Judas swing**, not a reversal (if bias is bearish)
4. At the rebalance level, take the [FVG](fair-value-gaps.md) + [MSS](market-structure-shift.md) + [displacement](displacement.md) entry
5. Target the opposite [draw on liquidity](draw-on-liquidity.md)
