## What is Risk On / Risk Off?

A **market regime** distinction driven by the **US Dollar**:

| Regime | Dollar | Capital flow | Risk assets (FX, indices) |
|--------|--------|--------------|---------------------------|
| **Risk OFF** | Dollar **up** | Flight to quality / safe haven **into** the dollar | **Fall** |
| **Risk ON** | Dollar **down** | Capital **out** of the dollar into risk assets | **Rise** |

> A **rising dollar** = **risk-off**: money pours **into** the dollar (safe haven) and **out of** risk assets → foreign currencies and stock indices decline. A **falling dollar** = **risk-on**: the opposite.

---

## Dollar Directional Correlation

```
   Dollar ▲  →  foreign currencies ▼  ,  stock indices ▼   (RISK OFF)
   Dollar ▼  →  foreign currencies ▲  ,  stock indices ▲   (RISK ON)
```

- The dollar's direction **inversely drives** foreign currencies (see [Intermarket Correlation](intermarket-correlation.md))
- A **rising dollar pressures** currencies → sustained rallies less likely, declines more likely
- Same logic extends to **stock indices** (e.g., DXY vs E-mini S&P move inversely)

---

## Why It Matters

- Sets the **macro backdrop** for your [daily bias](daily-bias.md): if the dollar is bullish (risk-off), lean **short** risk assets
- Explains why FX pairs and indices **weaken together** when the dollar is strong
- One coherent **intermarket narrative** ties currencies + indices to the dollar's regime
- Combine with **seasonal tendencies** (e.g., a seasonally weak May) for extra conviction

---

## How to Use It

1. Read the **Dollar Index** direction/bias first
2. Dollar bullish → **risk-off** → bias risk assets (FX, indices) **down**
3. Dollar bearish → **risk-on** → bias risk assets **up**
4. Confirm with the specific [inverse correlations](intermarket-correlation.md) (DXY↔EUR, DXY↔ES)
5. Then drill to the [draw on liquidity](draw-on-liquidity.md) and execute the intraday model
