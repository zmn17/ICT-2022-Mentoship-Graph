## What is a Breaker?

A breaker is a specific swing structure — **a high, a low, and a higher high with a net return** — that, once formed, acts as a support/resistance zone and gives a **precise stop-placement reference**. It marks a failed move that "breaks" and reverses, leaving a clean level to trade against.

---

## The Structure

```
                    higher high
                       ╱╲        ← price makes a higher high…
        high          ╱  ╲
         ╱╲          ╱    ╲
        ╱  ╲        ╱      ╲  ← …then returns (net return)
   ────╱    ╲──────╱        
             ╲____ low        ← the low between the two highs
```

Three reference points:
1. **High** — an initial swing high
2. **Low** — the pullback low after it
3. **Higher high** — price makes a higher high, then **returns** back through

The zone formed by this structure becomes the **breaker** — price reacts to it on the return.

---

## Why It Matters

- Provides a **defined, structural stop** — set stops **just above the breaker candle** (for shorts) to limit risk
- Marks where a move **failed and reversed**, so it's a logical support/resistance on the retest
- Complements [order blocks](order-blocks.md) and [fair value gaps](fair-value-gaps.md) as an entry/stop reference

---

## Using the Breaker for Stops

> Set stops **just above the breaker candle**, keeping risk tightly defined.

- For a **bearish** setup: stop just above the breaker's high
- For a **bullish** setup: stop just below the breaker's low
- The breaker gives a **cleaner, tighter** stop than an arbitrary distance

---

## How to Trade It

1. Identify the breaker structure (high → low → higher high → net return)
2. Wait for price to **return** into the breaker zone
3. Look for confluence: a [fair value gap](fair-value-gaps.md) or [order block](order-blocks.md) at the same level
4. Enter on the retest; place the **stop just beyond the breaker candle**
5. Target the next [liquidity](liquidity.md) pool or [FVG](fair-value-gaps.md)

---

## Breaker vs. Order Block

| | [Order Block](order-blocks.md) | Breaker |
|---|---|---|
| Formation | Cluster of same-direction candles before displacement | High → low → higher high with a net return |
| Primary use | Entry zone (delivery-state change) | Stop reference + reversal zone on a failed move |
| Both | Institutional support/resistance levels for entries & stops | |
