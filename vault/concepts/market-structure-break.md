## What is a Market Structure Break?

A Market Structure Break (also called MSS or Break of Structure / BOS) occurs when price **breaks a short-term swing low or swing high**, signaling continuation (or shift) in the trend direction. It is the **confirmation** that the anticipated move is underway.

> Not to be confused with [Market Structure (Premium/Discount)](market-structure.md), which is about where price sits within a range. This concept is about the **break** of a swing point.

---

## Bearish Break of Structure

```
     ╱╲          ← Short-term high
    ╱  ╲    ╱╲
   ╱    ╲  ╱  ╲
  ╱      ╲╱    ╲
              ╲
  ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─╲─ ─ ─  Short-term low
                ╲
                 ▼  ← Price BREAKS below the low
                     = Bearish break of structure
                     = Confirms continuation of downtrend
```

When price **breaks a short-term low**, it confirms bearish continuation — aligning with a bearish [weekly bias](multi-timeframe-analysis.md).

---

## Bullish Break of Structure

When price **breaks a short-term high**, it confirms bullish continuation — aligning with a bullish [weekly bias](multi-timeframe-analysis.md).

---

## The Break → Retrace → Enter Sequence

This is the critical part. **Do not enter on the break itself.**

```
1. BREAK    → Price breaks the short-term low (bearish BOS)
              This leaves behind a Fair Value Gap (imbalance)
                        │
2. RETRACE  → Price retraces UP into the FVG / imbalance zone
                        │
3. ENTER    → Short entry on the retest of the imbalance
              (NOT on the break — avoids chasing & slippage)
```

> "It's important to enter short positions when price **retests the imbalance** after breaking the structure, **not simply on the break**, to avoid chasing price or execution slippage."

---

## Why the Break Matters

- The break **confirms** the higher-timeframe bias is playing out
- It signals that [liquidity](liquidity.md) has been taken and the algorithm is repricing
- The displacement that causes the break typically leaves a [Fair Value Gap](fair-value-gaps.md) — your entry zone
- A break aligned with the [weekly bias](multi-timeframe-analysis.md) is a high-probability continuation signal

---

## Where to Spot It

Per the top-down framework, the break of structure is identified on the **2-minute** (or 15-minute) chart — after the higher timeframes have set bias and mapped liquidity. See [Multi-Timeframe Analysis](multi-timeframe-analysis.md).

---

## Entry & Risk

| Element | Placement |
|---------|-----------|
| **Entry** | On retracement into the [FVG](fair-value-gaps.md) after the break |
| **Stop (tight)** | Above the imbalance zone's high |
| **Stop (conservative)** | Above the high of the candle that broke the short-term low |

Stop choice depends on personal risk tolerance. See [Risk Management](risk-management.md).

---

## Part of the "Setup Fingerprints"

A break of structure is one of the repeatable "fingerprints" of a valid setup:

1. A run on the opposite side's stops ([stop hunt](stop-hunts.md))
2. **A market structure break confirming continuation** ← this concept
3. Price retracing into an [imbalance](fair-value-gaps.md) before continuation
