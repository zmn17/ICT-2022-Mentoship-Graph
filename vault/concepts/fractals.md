## What is a Fractal?

A fractal is a pattern that **repeats on smaller and higher timeframes** in a similar — though **not identical** — form. The same [imbalance → rebalance](fair-value-gaps.md) behavior, the same [market structure hierarchy](market-structure-hierarchy.md), and the same [order block](order-blocks.md) mechanics show up at every scale.

> "A fractal is something that repeats on a smaller or higher timeframe that's similar in its formation; it won't be identical but it's closely related to the general idea."

---

## Why It Matters

- The **daily** fair value gap has the **same structure** as a one-candle gap on the 15-minute
- Lets you **drill down** (daily → hourly → 15/5/4-min) and find the *same* logic to refine entries
- A small imbalance on the 15-minute rebalancing = an **aggressive entry** mirroring the hourly-level idea
- Explains why one model works across all timeframes and instruments

```
   DAILY:     ┌──────┐  large imbalance
              │ FVG  │
              └──────┘
                 ↓ same pattern, smaller
   15-MIN:      ┌──┐   one-candle imbalance
                │FVG│
                └──┘
```

---

## How to Use Fractals

1. Establish the setup on the **higher timeframe** (daily/hourly)
2. **Drop down** — expect the same imbalance/rebalance/structure to appear
3. Use the lower-timeframe fractal to get a **tighter, earlier entry** ([OTE](optimal-trade-entry.md))
4. Keep the higher-timeframe **context** as the parent — the fractal must agree with it
