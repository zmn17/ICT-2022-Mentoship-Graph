## What is Tape Reading?

Tape reading is executing in **real time** off **narrative and order flow** rather than rigid rules — anticipating *where other traders' stops rest* and *how price should behave*, then managing the position by that story instead of a fixed mechanical stop. It layers on top of structure ([FVG](fair-value-gaps.md), [MSS](market-structure-shift.md)) to time entries and manage risk live.

---

## The Core Idea

> Other traders buying the same [fair value gap](fair-value-gaps.md) will rest their **sell stops below the swing low** (e.g., 13,614). So a brief **poke below** that takes those stops is **expected, not feared** — it "does not negate the fair value gap" because you're trading the **narrative**.

- You *predict* the stop run and let it happen **to your benefit**
- A wick below the low that runs stops and reverses = confirmation, not invalidation
- The story ("price should be drawn up toward X") governs the trade

---

## Managing Without a Hard Stop

At high-volatility moments (the **9:30 open**, news), stops get run easily. Tape readers may:
- Manage risk **manually** — "finger on the close button" — rather than leaving a resting stop that gets picked off
- Exit by **narrative**: if price stops behaving as the story requires, close it
- This is an **advanced** technique — it requires discipline and full attention; a beginner should use a defined stop

> ⚠️ Trading without a hard stop is high-risk and only appropriate with strict discipline and constant attention. Default to a defined stop unless you have the experience to manage live.

---

## Tape Reading + Structure

```
Bias (daily) → wait for MSS → FVG forms → narrative: where are the stops?
→ expect a poke that runs them → enter as price re-enters the FVG
→ manage by story (finger on close) → exit at target / when narrative breaks
```
