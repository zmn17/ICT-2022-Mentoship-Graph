## What is an Optimal Trade Entry?

An Optimal Trade Entry (OTE) is entering **as close as possible to the origin of the expected move** — near the important high (for a short) or low (for a long) — rather than **chasing** a breakout. It maximizes reward-to-risk by keeping the stop tight and letting the full move deliver.

---

## The Principle

> "I'm **not** waiting for a breakout below that low — I'm getting up in there where it's an **optimal entry**." … "You don't need the highest high, but you need to understand **why price should trade away** from the level you're entering at."

- Enter into the **[fair value gap](fair-value-gaps.md)** near the significant swing high/low
- Do **not** chase price after it has already displaced — that inflates risk
- The closer to the origin, the **tighter the stop** and the **bigger the R:R**

---

## Where OTE Sits

```
   important high (intermediate-term)  ← enter as close as possible
        ┌──────────┐
        │   FVG    │  ← OTE zone: short into the premium retrace
        └──────────┘
             │  wait for displacement to confirm
             ▼  let it run to the draw on liquidity
```

Classic OTE is a **premium retracement** into the fair value gap (often the deeper Fib retracement zone) — a discount price to buy, or a premium price to sell.

### The OTE Retracement Zone (62–79%)

The textbook OTE is the **0.62–0.79 retracement** of the leg (the **62%, 70.5%, 79%** Fib levels). Enter as price retraces into this zone — often overlapping a [fair value gap](fair-value-gaps.md) / [order block](order-blocks.md) — for the deepest discount (long) or richest premium (short) with the tightest stop just beyond the origin.

---

## Why It Works

- **Risk is defined and small** — stop just beyond the swing high/low
- Reward is large — the move runs to the [draw on liquidity](draw-on-liquidity.md)
- Avoids the trap of **chasing** after [displacement](displacement.md) has already happened
- Confirmed by [displacement](displacement.md) leaving the zone in your favor

---

## How to Take an OTE

1. Identify the significant [swing high/low](swing-points.md) (ideally intermediate/long-term)
2. Mark the [fair value gap](fair-value-gaps.md) near it
3. Place a **limit** in the gap for entry on the retrace — don't chase
4. Stop just beyond the swing point
5. Wait for [displacement](displacement.md) to confirm; target the [draw on liquidity](draw-on-liquidity.md)
