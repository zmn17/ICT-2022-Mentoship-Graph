## What is Intermarket Correlation?

Correlated instruments usually move together. When they **agree**, they confirm your bias; when they **diverge**, the odd one out often reveals what smart money is really doing. ICT uses this across the stock indices (NASDAQ, S&P, Dow) and across Forex crosses (via the underlying currencies).

---

## Stock Index Correlation (NASDAQ / S&P / Dow)

| Instrument | Role |
|------------|------|
| **NASDAQ (NQ)** | Primary trading vehicle |
| **S&P 500 (ES)** | Usually moves in step with NASDAQ — confirmation |
| **Dow (YM)** | Macro tell; often **leads or diverges** from NQ/ES |

### Confirmation
If a [market structure shift](market-structure-shift.md) + [FVG](fair-value-gaps.md) appear in the **S&P** but not yet in NASDAQ, the S&P can **guide entry timing** on NASDAQ.

### Divergence = the "Lack of Delivery" Tell
> When **NASDAQ and S&P make a new low but the Dow does NOT**, that divergence signals **accumulation** and a likely **fake break lower** → expect a rally.

This "lack of delivery" — one index refusing to deliver price to a new low/high — is a high-value warning that the break is a liquidity grab, not a real move. See [Stop Hunts](stop-hunts.md).

```
   NASDAQ:  lower low  ✔  (breaks old low)
   S&P:     lower low  ✔
   DOW:     NO lower low ✘  ← divergence / lack of delivery
                            = fake break → accumulation → rally
```

## Forex Cross Correlation

For a cross like **EUR/JPY**, read the **two underlying currencies** instead of the cross alone:

- **Euro strength** — confirmed via the **6E** (Euro FX futures, continuous front month) showing an uptrend
- **Yen weakness** — Yen consolidating/weak
- Euro strong **+** Yen weak → **bullish bias on EUR/JPY**

This correlative approach is essential for **exotic or less-obvious crosses** where the pair's own chart gives an unclear bias. See [Daily Bias](daily-bias.md).

## Inverse Correlation — the Dollar Index Barometer (Ep 20)

Some instruments move **inversely**. The **US Dollar Index (DXY)** and **EUR** are a **teeter-totter**:

```
   DXY ▲  ⟷  EUR ▼
   DXY ▼  ⟷  EUR ▲
```

- If **EUR** is your trading market, use the **DXY as a barometer** to *measure and confirm* EUR's move
- Example (Ep 20): DXY dropped to an engineered **99.92** buy level at the **exact instant** EUR took out the previous week's high → that EUR breakout was **false** (a [Judas swing](judas-swing.md))
- The inverse instrument **times and validates** the setup: when the dollar hits its objective, the euro's opposing extreme is suspect
- Same logic for other inversely-correlated pairs (e.g., USD/CHF vs EUR/USD)

---

## Why It Works

- A single chart can be manipulated with a [stop hunt](stop-hunts.md); **three correlated charts are harder to fake in unison**
- The instrument that **fails to confirm** exposes the true intent (accumulation/distribution)
- Confirmation across instruments **raises probability**; divergence **warns** of a trap

---

## How to Use It

1. Establish [daily bias](daily-bias.md) on your primary instrument
2. Pull up the **correlated instruments** (indices: NQ/ES/YM; Forex: the two currencies)
3. **Confirmation:** all agree → trade with confidence, use the leader to time entries
4. **Divergence:** one refuses to make the new high/low → suspect a fake break; fade it
5. Combine with [FVG](fair-value-gaps.md) / [MSS](market-structure-shift.md) on the primary for the entry
