## Why the Economic Calendar Matters

Most traders ignore the economic calendar — a mistake. Scheduled news is the **fuel and timing** for volatility. It tells you **when** algorithms are likely to move price and run liquidity, which is half of the [time & price](market-efficiency-paradigm.md) equation.

---

## Impact Levels (ForexFactory)

Using a calendar like **forexfactory.com**, classify events by impact:

| Impact | Color | Meaning |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **High** | 🔴 Red | Major volatility expected — highest risk & opportunity |
| **Medium** | 🟠 Orange | Moderate potential to move price |
| **Low** | 🟡 Yellow | Minor / usually ignorable |

## The 8:30 AM Anchor

- **8:30 AM ET** is the key daily observation point — news embargoes lift and market-moving data floods in
- Expect **rapid, unexpected fluctuations** around releases
- This aligns with the [session](trading-sessions.md) morning window and is a prime **time-based** liquidity event

### The 2:00 PM Fed Window (Ep 30)

- **FOMC statement / Fed Chair press conference** land at **2:00 PM ET** ("jawboning")
- Drives **afternoon (PM) volatility** — price manipulates around the news, sweeping liquidity before the real move
- Often follows a **[narrow-range day](narrow-range-day.md)** prelude the day before
- Post-2:00, algorithms may **[spool](algorithmic-programs.md)** price toward the true draw (e.g., after 3:00)

## Catalyst vs. Smokescreen

News plays two roles — knowing which is happening is the edge:

| Role | What Happens |
|------|--------------|
| **Catalyst** | The data genuinely drives a directional move |
| **Smokescreen** | The release is cover to trigger a [liquidity run](stop-hunts.md) / [Judas swing](judas-swing.md) before reversing |

> Often the initial spike on news is a **smokescreen** — a stop hunt that traps reactive traders before price delivers in the real (bias) direction.

---

## Trading Around News

- Trading **around releases = heightened risk** (fast, whippy moves)
- Keep **stops wide enough** to survive the initial volatility spike / "pump"
- Let the news-driven [liquidity](liquidity.md) grab happen, then trade the resolution in the direction of your [daily bias](daily-bias.md)
- Use the calendar to **schedule attention**, not to predict direction — direction comes from structure & bias

---

## How to Use It

1. Each morning, scan the calendar; note **high-impact** events and their times
2. Anchor on **8:30 AM ET** for the main data window
3. Decide in advance: will you **stand aside** through the release or trade its resolution?
4. Treat the first spike as a possible **smokescreen** — wait for the [FVG](fair-value-gaps.md) / structure to confirm
5. Align the post-news move with your [daily bias](daily-bias.md)

---

## The FOMC / NFP Staged Reaction (Ep 23)

High-impact events (FOMC, Nonfarm Payroll) deliver price in **staged legs**, not one clean move:

```
   1. Kneejerk leg   →  2. another leg  →  3. FAKE-OUT leg  →  4. the REAL setup
```

- **Wait out** the initial shakeout — don't trade the first spike
- The early legs are **noise** engineered by news volatility (often a [Judas swing](judas-swing.md))
- The genuine, tradeable setup comes **after** the fake-out — often a retrace into a higher-timeframe [FVG](fair-value-gaps.md)
