## What is the Sunday Gap Opening?

When the futures session **re-opens Sunday night**, it usually **gaps** from Friday's close. That gap becomes a **primary reference level for the entire week** — a **magnet** and **decision level** that price returns to and reacts against, not just a Monday event.

---

## Extend It Through the Whole Week

> Take the **Sunday gap opening** and **extend it across the entire week.**

- **Consolidation weeks** (e.g., **FOMC** weeks): price **keeps coming back** to interact with the level
- **Strong trending weeks**: price may **run away** from it and never return
- Treat it as a **magnet + decision level**, re-tested repeatedly

---

## Gap Flips Support ↔ Resistance

```
   Sunday gap opening ═══════
        │  first acts as SUPPORT
        ▼  price trades DOWN through it (+ sell-side)
   ─── below ───
        ▲  price comes back UP to it
   Sunday gap now acts as RESISTANCE (repeated retests)
```

Once price trades **below** the Sunday gap and returns, the gap **flips** from support to **resistance** (mirror for the bullish case). Classic level-polarity, but anchored to the **weekly opening gap**.

---

## How to Trade Around It

1. Mark the **Sunday-night opening gap** (from Friday's close)
2. **Extend the level** across the whole week
3. Use it as a **draw** (magnet) and a **support/resistance** decision point
4. Combine with [FVGs](fair-value-gaps.md) and [order blocks](order-blocks.md) for the actual entries as price stair-steps toward/through it
5. In a **consolidation/FOMC week**, expect **repeated returns**; in a trending week, expect a one-way run
6. Project the next **draw** into an **unfilled higher/lower gap** beyond it
