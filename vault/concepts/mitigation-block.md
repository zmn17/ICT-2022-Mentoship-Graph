## What is a Mitigation Block?

A mitigation block is an [order block](order-blocks.md)-like zone that price **returns to in order to "mitigate" (offset) trapped positions** before continuing in the intended direction. Smart money uses the revisit to let earlier, offside participants get out near break-even — and to fill fresh positions — before the real expansion.

---

## How It Forms

```
   1. Move fails / traps traders at a level
   2. Price reverses away
   3. Price RETURNS to that origin zone (the mitigation block)
      → trapped shorts/longs exit near break-even here
      → smart money fills fresh positions
   4. Price expands in the intended direction
```

- On a **bullish** turn: price returns down to the block, mitigates trapped longs, then expands up
- Often pairs with an **imbalance** ([FVG](fair-value-gaps.md)) at the same zone for the entry

---

## Mitigation Block vs. Order Block vs. Breaker

| | [Order Block](order-blocks.md) | [Breaker](breaker.md) | Mitigation Block |
|---|---|---|---|
| Origin | Last consecutive up/down closes before displacement | High-low-higher-high failed swing | Zone price returns to *mitigate* trapped positions |
| Use | Primary entry zone | Stop reference / reversal | Entry on the revisit that offsets trapped orders |

All three are institutional zones; the mitigation block emphasizes the **offsetting of trapped positions** on the return.

---

## How to Trade It

1. Identify where a prior move **trapped** traders (failed high/low)
2. Mark the origin zone as the **mitigation block**
3. Wait for price to **return** into it (often with an [FVG](fair-value-gaps.md) overlap)
4. Enter in the intended direction as trapped positions mitigate
5. Target the [draw on liquidity](draw-on-liquidity.md) (e.g., premium buy stops)
