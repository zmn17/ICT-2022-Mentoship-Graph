## What is SMT Divergence?

SMT (Smart Money Technique/Tool) Divergence is a **confirmation tool** where two **closely correlated** markets **disagree** at a key moment: one makes a **higher high** (or lower low) while the other **fails to**. That non-confirmation exposes the extreme as a **stop run**, and reveals underlying **accumulation or distribution** — all from **pure price**, no indicator.

---

## How It Works

```
   NASDAQ:  ──────╱╲  higher high  (pushes up)
   S&P:     ────╱╲    LOWER high   (fails to confirm)  ← DIVERGENCE
                       = S&P is weak → distribution → bearish confirmation
```

- Two markets that **normally move in tandem** (e.g., S&P 500 ↔ NASDAQ 100) are expected to mirror each other
- When one makes a new high/low the other **can't match**, the correlation **cracks**
- The **non-confirming** market is the **weaker/stronger** vehicle:
  - Bearish: one makes a **higher high**, the other a **lower high** → the higher high is a **stop run** → **distribution**
  - Bullish: one makes a **lower low**, the other a **higher low** → the lower low is a stop run → **accumulation**

---

## Why It Matters

- **Confirmation without an indicator** — it's raw price across correlated instruments
- An **"x-ray view"** of real accumulation/distribution
- Confirms your directional [bias](daily-bias.md) and which instrument to trade (the **weaker** one for shorts, **stronger** for longs)
- Requires **[intermarket correlation](intermarket-correlation.md)** to be valid — you must first expect the markets to mirror

---

## The Compare-and-Contrast Overlay

To make SMT visible on one screen:
1. Put one market on the chart (e.g., NASDAQ)
2. Use the platform's **compare/contrast** feature to add the correlated market (e.g., ES) in a new pane
3. Open settings → change the **price source to "High"** (for a high-to-high read) or "Low"
4. The high-to-high (or low-to-low) relationship now reads like an **indicator divergence** — except it's **pure price**

> "It looks like a divergence you'd see with an indicator, except it's not an indicator — it's price."

---

## Where to Use It

- The index family: **ES / NQ / YM** — a three-market compare lets one lagging market flag the true order flow
- Forex correlated pairs (or a pair vs its [DXY barometer](intermarket-correlation.md))
- At **key swing points** (session highs/lows, [draws on liquidity](draw-on-liquidity.md)) — that's where divergence is meaningful
