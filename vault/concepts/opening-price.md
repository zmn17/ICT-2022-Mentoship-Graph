## What Are the Opening-Price References?

ICT uses specific **opening prices** as directional reference lines. Two matter most, each for a different scope:

| Reference | Time (NY) | Scope |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **Midnight open** | 00:00 | The **entire daily range** |
| **8:30 AM open** | 08:30 | The **morning session** |

> "If you're looking at the **entire daily range** you use the **midnight** open; if you're looking at the **morning session** you use the **8:30** open."

---

## How to Use Them

- Price **above** the reference open = bullish framing for that scope; **below** = bearish
- Being **bearish below the 8:30 open** frames intent to trade down toward a lower [draw on liquidity](draw-on-liquidity.md)
- The midnight open frames the **whole day's** premium/discount lean
- A move above the open early that then reverses is often a [Judas swing](judas-swing.md)

```
   ── 8:30 open ──────────────  ← morning-session reference
        │ price above = intraday bullish framing
        │ price below = intraday bearish framing
   ── midnight open ──────────  ← whole-day reference
```

---

## Why Two References?

They answer different questions:
- **Midnight open:** "What's the bias for the *day*?"
- **8:30 open:** "What's the bias for the *morning session* I'm about to trade?"

Using the wrong one for the wrong scope is a common source of confusion — match the reference to your timeframe/scope.
