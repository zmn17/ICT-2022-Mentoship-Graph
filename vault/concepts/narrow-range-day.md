## What is a Narrow Range Day?

A narrow range day is a **low-volatility, small-range** session — choppy, two-sided price action with limited expansion. It commonly occurs **the day before a high-impact event** (e.g., the day before the Fed Chair speaks / FOMC), when the market coils rather than trends.

---

## Characteristics

- **Small daily range**, limited displacement
- **Choppy, two-sided** action that shakes out impatient traders
- Often precedes a **high-impact catalyst** (the market "waits")
- Demands **more experience** — ICT does **not** recommend trading directly ahead of the Fed

---

## Why Bias Matters *More* Here

> On a narrow-range day the **pre-established [daily bias](daily-bias.md) becomes even more important** — it prevents you from being **shaken out** by the choppy two-sided price action.

- Set the bias **before** the session (from the [draw on liquidity](draw-on-liquidity.md))
- **Filter out every counter-bias setup** — if bullish, take **only** longs
- The chop will *look* like reversals; the bias keeps you aligned with the true direction

---

## How to Trade One

1. Establish a clear **bias** from a draw on liquidity (e.g., relative equal highs above → bullish)
2. Trade **only** setups that agree with the bias — ignore the noise
3. Use **[SMT divergence](smt-divergence.md)** to confirm (e.g., NQ lower low while ES refuses = bullish)
4. Wait for a **short-term market structure shift** and a **1-minute [fair value gap](fair-value-gaps.md)**
5. **Close-proximity entries** are acceptable — a slightly imprecise fill near the gap is fine (there may be ~8 candles of opportunity)
6. Target the [liquidity](liquidity.md) resting in the bias direction
7. Consider **standing aside** entirely right before the Fed — narrow-range/pre-news trading is advanced
