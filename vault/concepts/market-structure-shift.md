## What is a Market Structure Shift?

A Market Structure Shift (MSS) is an **intraday change in the market's directional bias**, triggered when price takes out a key liquidity level and then reverses. It signals that short-term order flow has flipped.

---

## MSS vs. Break of Structure — Don't Confuse Them

> **Critical distinction from Episode 3:** "Market structure break" and "market structure shift" should **not** be used interchangeably.

| | [Market Structure Break (BOS)](market-structure-break.md) | Market Structure Shift (MSS) |
|---|---|---|
| **Timeframe** | Longer-term | Short-term / intraday |
| **Implication** | Multi-day directional move | Intraday change in bias |
| **Meaning** | Continuation of established trend | A flip in short-term direction, often after a liquidity sweep |
| **Where seen** | Higher timeframes (daily/hourly) | 15-min down to 1-min |

Think of a **break** as confirming the bigger trend continues; a **shift** as an intraday pivot after stops are run.

## How an MSS Forms

```
   relative equal highs (buy stops)
   ══════════════════════════════  ← 1. price runs UP, sweeps buy stops
              ╱╲                        (liquidity taken above old highs)
             ╱  ╲                   ← 2. price fails to continue,
            ╱    ╲                       makes a LOWER HIGH
   ────────╱      ╲──────              
                   ╲                 ← 3. price breaks a short-term low
   ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─╲─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─         = MARKET STRUCTURE SHIFT
                     ▼                    (intraday bias now bearish)
```

1. Price sweeps a liquidity pool (e.g., buy stops above [relative equal highs](internal-range-liquidity.md))
2. It fails to continue and prints a lower high
3. It then breaks a short-term swing low → the shift is confirmed
4. The intraday bias is now bearish; expect a move toward the opposite liquidity

---

## What Triggers a Shift

An MSS happens when price action moves **through key liquidity zones**:

- Sell stops below old lows (downside liquidity hunt)
- Buy stops above [relative equal highs](internal-range-liquidity.md) (upside liquidity hunt)

When these stops are taken intraday, it's a sign **liquidity is being run** — often immediately followed by the structure shift. See [Stop Hunts](stop-hunts.md).

---

## Trading the Shift

1. Identify the [internal range liquidity](internal-range-liquidity.md) — old lows, relative equal highs
2. Wait for a [stop hunt](stop-hunts.md) to sweep one side
3. Watch for price to fail and shift (lower high + break of short-term low, or vice versa)
4. Confirm on the 3-, 2-, or 1-minute chart with a [Fair Value Gap](fair-value-gaps.md) and/or [order block](order-blocks.md)
5. Enter on the retest; target the opposite liquidity pool or the [equilibrium (50%)](market-structure.md) level

---

## Confirming the Shift on Lower Timeframes

Per Episode 3, use the **3-, 2-, and 1-minute** charts to confirm the shift with hard evidence:

- A [Fair Value Gap](fair-value-gaps.md) left by the displacement
- An [order block](order-blocks.md) marking the change in [state of delivery](state-of-delivery.md)
- Short-term swing highs/lows showing the momentum of the liquidity takedown
