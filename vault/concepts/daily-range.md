## What is the Daily Range?

The daily trading range is the price territory covered in a session. Episode 5 frames it as **two major moves**: a **morning move** and an **afternoon move**. Understanding how these two combine gives you a directional bias and realistic intraday targets.

---

## The Two Moves

```
   MORNING MOVE                AFTERNOON MOVE
   (8:30 AM – Noon)            (1:00 PM – Close)
   ───────────────            ───────────────
   bullish / bearish /   →    continues OR reverses
   consolidation              the morning trend
```

| Move | Session | Character |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **Morning** | 8:30 AM – Noon | Sets the day's initial direction: bullish, bearish, or consolidation |
| **Afternoon** | 1:00 PM – Close | Continues or reverses the morning; driven by [algorithmic programs](algorithmic-programs.md) |

See [Trading Sessions](trading-sessions.md) for the session timing detail.

---

## Possible Daily Outcomes

| Scenario | Morning | Afternoon | Result |
|----------|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Measured move** | Bullish | Bullish (equal or greater) | Afternoon extends the trend by ≥ the morning's size |
| **Reversal** | Bullish | Reverses down | Afternoon undoes the morning |
| **Breakout** | Consolidation | Breakout | Range in the morning, expansion in the afternoon |

*(Mirror these for bearish mornings.)*

---

## Measured Moves

When morning and afternoon move the **same direction**, the afternoon leg often travels a distance **equal to or greater than** the morning leg:

```
   morning leg  = X points
   afternoon leg ≥ X points  (measured move projection)
```

Use the morning leg's size to **project** a realistic afternoon target.

---

## Trend Context (Framing the Session)

- Always frame the day within the **trend context of the previous day(s)**
- Read **accumulation vs. distribution** from swing structure:
  - **Higher lows = accumulation** (buyers stepping in → bias up)
  - **Lower highs = distribution** (sellers stepping in → bias down)

---

## Sentiment Lags Fundamentals

> Market sentiment often **lags** fundamentals. Even if the crowd expects price to continue *down*, quiet **accumulation** and [stop hunts](stop-hunts.md) may drive price *higher*.

This is why you read **order flow and structure**, not headlines — accumulation beneath the surface precedes the move.

---

## How to Use the Daily Range

1. Classify the **morning move** (bullish / bearish / consolidation)
2. Note the previous day(s) **trend context**
3. Read swing structure for **accumulation (higher lows)** or **distribution (lower highs)**
4. After the lunch lull, anticipate the **afternoon move** — continuation (measured move) or reversal
5. Project targets using the **morning leg size** and the opposite [liquidity](liquidity.md) pools
