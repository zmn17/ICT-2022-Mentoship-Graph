## What is Top-Down Analysis?

Multi-timeframe (top-down) analysis is the practice of forming a directional bias on a **high timeframe** and then progressively drilling down to lower timeframes to refine levels and pinpoint precise entries. Each timeframe answers a different question.

```
WEEKLY   → What is the bias? (higher or lower this week?)
   ↓
DAILY    → Where are the swing highs/lows and liquidity pools?
   ↓
HOURLY   → Where do those levels sit at finer resolution? Watch stop hunts.
   ↓
15-MIN   → Confirm the higher-timeframe structure and liquidity.
   ↓
2-MIN    → Find the imbalance (FVG) and market structure break.
   ↓
1-MIN    → Execute with precision.
```

---

## The Timeframe Hierarchy

| Timeframe | Purpose | What You Look For |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| **Weekly** | Establish bias | Will the weekly candle likely move higher or lower from its open? |
| **Daily** | Map liquidity | Swing highs/lows (stop clusters), imbalances |
| **Hourly** | Refine levels | Transpose daily highs/lows; observe [stop hunts](stop-hunts.md) |
| **15-min** | Confirm structure | Validate higher-timeframe structure and [liquidity](liquidity.md) |
| **2-min** | Find the setup | Precise [FVGs](fair-value-gaps.md) and [market structure breaks](market-structure-break.md) |
| **1-min** | Execute | Sniper entry on the retest |

---

## Establishing Weekly Bias

The starting point of the entire framework:

- Form your directional bias **over the weekend**, before the new week begins
- The goal is **NOT** to predict the exact weekly close
- The goal is to estimate whether price will likely move **higher or lower** from the weekly open
- Think of price as being **magnetically drawn** toward key levels and [liquidity](liquidity.md) pools, influenced by seasonality and major price points

> "Trading success starts with a clear weekly directional bias, emphasizing **probability over certainty**."

This weekly bias is the **premise** every lower-timeframe decision must align with. If your weekly bias is bearish, you look for shorts on the lower timeframes — not longs.

---

## How Each Level Feeds the Next

1. **Weekly** sets the directional premise (e.g., bearish this week)
2. **Daily** shows where you currently sit within the weekly's expected range expansion, and where liquidity rests
3. **Hourly** refines the daily levels and reveals engineered [stop hunts](stop-hunts.md)
4. **15-min** confirms the structure holds at finer resolution
5. **2-min** reveals the [imbalance](fair-value-gaps.md) and the [break of structure](market-structure-break.md) that trigger entry
6. **1-min** lets you execute the entry on the retest with minimal risk

---

## Why This Works

- Lower timeframes without higher-timeframe context are **noise**
- Higher timeframes without lower-timeframe refinement give **poor entries** (too much risk)
- The top-down approach aligns your entry with **institutional / algorithmic** order flow across scales
- It turns a vague "I think it goes down" into a **precise, low-risk, high-probability** short entry

---

## Common Mistakes

- ❌ Starting on the 2-min chart without a weekly bias → trading noise
- ❌ Predicting exact price targets instead of directional probability
- ❌ Taking a long on the 2-min when the weekly bias is bearish
- ❌ Entering on the structure break instead of waiting for the retest (see [Market Structure Break](market-structure-break.md))
