## What This Episode Teaches

A **real-money** long scalp on **FOMC day**, layering **tape reading** on top of the model: buy a fair value gap after a bullish MSS, anticipate the **sell stops** below a swing low being run (rather than fearing them), manage **without a hard stop** (finger on the close button at the volatile 9:30 open), and respect **FOMC discipline** — trade early, be done early. Result: **~$1,255 on two trades**.

```

4. Tape Reading        → Sell stops at 13,614 will be run; trade the narrative
5. Economic Calendar   → FOMC day: trade early, be done early
6. Risk Management     → Manual management, no resting stop at the open
```

## Key Lessons

- **Tape reading** turns a feared stop-run into an **expected** part of the plan — a poke below the low that runs sell stops then reverses is confirmation
- On **FOMC / 9:30**, resting stops get picked off easily → some traders manage **manually** (advanced; requires discipline)
- **FOMC discipline:** get your trade early and **step aside** before the afternoon volatility
- A realistic post-mentorship expectation framed as **~20%/month** "without pushing the envelope"

## The Core Insight

> On FOMC day, keep it simple and **early**: bullish bias → break the swing high → buy the **fair value gap**, and use **tape reading** to *expect* the sell-stop run below 13,614 rather than fear it. Manage the volatile open by narrative, target the premium FVG, book it, and step aside before the news.
