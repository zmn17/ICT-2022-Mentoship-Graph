## Daily Chart — The Narrative

```
   HIGH ─────────────────
        ╲  rally into premium, fails at prior high
        ┌────────┐
        │imbalance│ (FVG) ← price rebalanced here over 3 days
        └────────┘
   ── 50% equilibrium ──
        │  premium above / discount below
        ▼
   old low ═════════════  ← SELL-SIDE LIQUIDITY = the draw (magnet)
```

1. A daily **imbalance** was outlined (high of one candle → low of another). See [Fair Value Gaps](../../concepts/fair-value-gaps.md).
2. Price went discount → **premium**, failed above the prior high, broke back below **50% equilibrium**, and left a gap. See [Market Structure](../../concepts/market-structure.md).
3. **Sell-side liquidity below the old low** = the **draw on liquidity** — a magnet. Bias: **bearish**. See [Draw on Liquidity](../../concepts/draw-on-liquidity.md).
4. Three days of rebalancing, then **release lower** to attack the sell side.

---

## Morning — Framing the Short

- Used the **8:30 open** for the morning session (midnight open frames the whole day). Price **below 8:30 open** = bearish. See [Opening Price](../../concepts/opening-price.md).
- The morning rally was a **Judas swing** — a fake to lull buyers into chasing higher. See [Judas Swing](../../concepts/judas-swing.md).

```
   8:30 ─┐   ╱╲  ← Judas swing (fake rally)
         │  ╱  ╲
   ┌─────┴─┐╱    ╲___ real move: DOWN
   │  FVG  │       toward the draw
   └───────┘
```

> **Order-block trap:** novices saw a down-close candle and called it a "bullish order block" to buy. Wrong context — the real reference was the **imbalance above** to **short**. See [Order Blocks](../../concepts/order-blocks.md).

---

## Entry — Optimal, Near the Intermediate-Term High (4/3-min)

```
   LTH ◉
    ITH ◎ ─────────  ← enter as close as possible to this high
     STH ○  ┌──────┐
            │ FVG  │ ← short inside the gap (OTE), NOT chasing the low
            └──────┘
                │ displacement lower = confirmation
                ▼
```

1. Nested the swing highs — **STH ○ / ITH ◎ / LTH ◉** — confirming bearish structure. See [Swing Points](../../concepts/swing-points.md).
2. Took the **optimal entry** near the **intermediate-term high**, inside the fair value gap — *not* waiting for a breakout below the low. See [Optimal Trade Entry](../../concepts/optimal-trade-entry.md).
3. **Displacement** lower confirmed → sit back and let it work. See [Displacement](../../concepts/displacement.md).
4. Weighted candle **bodies over wicks** to define the gap ("price can color outside the lines").
5. **Fill: 10:33 AM**, entry **13,658.25**.

---

## Management & Exit — Into the Draw

- Monday was a **bank holiday** (session closed 1:00 PM) → held **open-ended** (no fixed limit) to let a fast move run.
- Anticipated a **gap-down** on the **6:00 PM reopen**; price slammed into the sell-side liquidity below the old daily low.
- **Cover: 13,612.25** → **+$492**.

```
   entry 13,658.25 ─┐
                    ▼ (held through holiday close)
   reopen gap-down ─┐
                    ▼ slam into sell-side draw
   cover 13,612.25  = +$492
```

---

## Aftermath (Next Session)

- The large reopen gap gets **rebalanced**; price consolidates around the old low.
- Next morning after 9:30 still **bearish bias** → another **FVG short**.
- For longer-term buyers: **relative equal lows** of sell-side liquidity below to attack. See [Liquidity](../../concepts/liquidity.md).

## The Repeatable Sequence

```
Daily draw (sell-side below old low) → premium retrace = sell chance
→ 8:30 bearish framing → Judas swing fake → nest swing highs (bearish)
→ optimal entry in FVG near ITH → displacement confirms
→ hold to the draw → cover into sell-side liquidity
```
