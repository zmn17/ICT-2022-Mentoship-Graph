## What This Episode Teaches

A screen-by-screen breakdown of **one real NQ short**, tying the daily-chart narrative (imbalance rebalanced, draw on sell-side liquidity below an old low) to a precise lower-timeframe execution at **10:33 AM**. It introduces several foundational tools: the **draw on liquidity** magnet, **nested swing-point** structure, **optimal trade entry**, and the **midnight vs 8:30 opening-price** references. Result: a **$492** win (13,658.25 → 13,612.25).

## The Entry Model (Step-by-Step)

### Stage 1 — Daily Narrative & Bias

| Step | Action | Concept |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | On the **daily**, mark the **imbalance** (high of one candle → low of another) and the dealing range. | [Fair Value Gaps](../../concepts/fair-value-gaps.md) / [Market Structure](../../concepts/market-structure.md) |
| 2 | Identify the **draw**: sell-side liquidity resting below an old low = the magnet. | [Draw on Liquidity](../../concepts/draw-on-liquidity.md) |
| 3 | Bias = **bearish** (toward the draw); retracements into premium are selling chances. | [Draw on Liquidity](../../concepts/draw-on-liquidity.md) |

### Stage 2 — Morning Framing

| Step | Action | Concept |
|------|--------|---------|
| 4 | Use the **8:30 open** for the morning session (midnight open frames the whole day). Bearish below 8:30. | [Opening Price](../../concepts/opening-price.md) |
| 5 | Read the morning rally as a **Judas swing** — a fake to lull buyers. | [Judas Swing](../../concepts/judas-swing.md) |
| 6 | Don't be fooled by a down-close "bullish order block" — the real context is the **imbalance above** to sell into. | [Fair Value Gaps](../../concepts/fair-value-gaps.md) / [Order Blocks](../../concepts/order-blocks.md) |

### Stage 3 — Structure & Entry (drop to 4/3-min)

| Step | Action | Concept |
|------|--------|---------|
| 7 | Nest the **swing highs** (STH → ITH → LTH) to confirm bearish structure. | [Swing Points](../../concepts/swing-points.md) |
| 8 | Take the **optimal entry** near the **intermediate-term high**, inside the fair value gap — don't chase the low. | [Optimal Trade Entry](../../concepts/optimal-trade-entry.md) |
| 9 | Wait for **displacement** lower to confirm; then hold. Fill @ **10:33 AM**. | [Displacement](../../concepts/displacement.md) |

### Stage 4 — Manage to the Draw

| Step | Action | Concept |
|------|--------|---------|
| 10 | Weight candle **bodies** over wicks when defining the gap ("price can color outside the lines"). | [Fair Value Gaps](../../concepts/fair-value-gaps.md) |
| 11 | Held **open-ended** (no limit) into the post-holiday reopen gap-down; covered into the sell-side draw. | [Draw on Liquidity](../../concepts/draw-on-liquidity.md) |
| 12 | **Discipline:** after a big winning move, don't rush back into the morning session. | [Trading Psychology](../../concepts/trading-psychology.md) |

## Order Block Misconception (Key Warning)

> A down-closed candle after a rally is **not** automatically a bullish order block. "Every down-close = buy" is wrong. An [order block](../../concepts/order-blocks.md) only matters in the correct **context** — here the real context was a bearish [draw on liquidity](../../concepts/draw-on-liquidity.md) and an [FVG](../../concepts/fair-value-gaps.md) to **sell** into.

## The Core Insight

> Higher-timeframe **narrative** (a draw on sell-side liquidity below an old low) dictates the trade; lower timeframes just **time** it. Read the morning pop as a Judas swing, nest your swing highs to confirm bearish, take the **optimal entry** near the intermediate-term high in the FVG, wait for **displacement**, and hold to the draw. Then have the discipline to stop after the win.
