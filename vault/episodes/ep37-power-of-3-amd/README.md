## What This Teaches

The **capstone**: a single trading day *is* the **Power of Three (AMD)** — **A**ccumulation → **M**anipulation → **D**istribution. The market **opens, accumulates, manipulates lower to create the low of the day, then distributes higher**. The **daily fair value gap** (which is also a **breaker**) anchors every lower-timeframe move — so the "noise" on the 1- and 5-minute charts is the **same daily AMD story on a smaller interval** (scale-independent).

## The Big Idea: Power of 3 is Scale-Independent

```
   DAILY:   open → accumulate → manipulate (low of day) → distribute (higher)
              ↓ same story, smaller interval
   5/1-MIN:  open → accumulate → manipulate → distribute
```

> "If you see this pattern on a **daily** chart it's easy to understand, but on lower timeframes it's easy to get lost in the candles. It's **not noise** — it's doing what it does on the daily chart." Every timeframe is the **same AMD story**. See [Power of 3](../../concepts/power-of-3.md) and [Fractals](../../concepts/fractals.md).

## The Core Insight (Series Capstone)

> A trading day **is** the **Power of Three**: it **opens, accumulates, manipulates** price down to set the **low of the day**, then **distributes** higher toward the **draw** (the daily FVG). This AMD rhythm is **scale-independent** — the chop on the 1- and 5-minute charts is the **same daily story**. Everything in the mentorship — liquidity, FVGs, order blocks, MSS, sessions — serves this one framework.
