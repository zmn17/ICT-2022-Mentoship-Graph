## What This Teaches

How a **pre-established bias governs a narrow-range (low-volatility) day**. The day before the Fed Chair speaks is choppy and small-range, so ICT — bullish off the **12553.25** NQ relative equal highs — **refuses every short** and hunts **only longs** at a **1-minute fair value gap**, using **SMT divergence** (NQ lower low vs ES refusing) and a short-term MSS. Close-proximity entries are acceptable.

## Why the Bias Rules a Narrow-Range Day

> With a bullish bias, "there were **no short trades** that lined up with the model today." When price took a short-term high and dropped, ICT was **simply not interested** in shorting. On a choppy, low-volatility day the bias is what **stops you being shaken out** by two-sided noise. See [Narrow Range Day](../../concepts/narrow-range-day.md).

## Quick-Reference Checklist

```
□ Bias set pre-session (bullish off 12553.25 equal highs + daily swing low)
□ Recognize narrow-range/low-vol context (day before Fed) — advanced; consider standing aside
□ Take ONLY bias-aligned setups (longs) — ignore the chop
□ SMT confirms (NQ lower low, ES refuses = bullish)
□ Failure to make a lower low → short-term MSS
□ Buy the 1-min FVG (close-proximity OK, ~8 candles)
□ 2nd MSS → expand through gaps into liquidity above
```

## The Core Insight

> On a **narrow-range day**, the **bias is everything**. Set it from the **draw on liquidity** (12553.25 equal highs = bullish), then **refuse all shorts** and let **SMT divergence** + a short-term **MSS** + a **1-minute FVG** time the long. The chop tries to shake you out — the bias keeps you aligned. (And ideally, don't trade *right before* the Fed.)
