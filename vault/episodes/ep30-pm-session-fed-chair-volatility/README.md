## What This Teaches

A **PM (afternoon) session** around a **2:00 PM Fed Chair** event, analyzed **bottom-up** (1-min first, out to daily). A **Judas swing** ran to **12535.5** — one tick short of the morning high (12535.75) — then broke lower to **sweep the NY lunch lows** (manipulation squeezing longs). The true **draw** was **12553.25** above; after 3:00 the algos **spooled** price up to hammer it. Key lesson: **selectivity** — the afternoon sell-off ran liquidity but gave **no valid model setup**; the bullish leg to 12553.25 did.

## The Selectivity Lesson

> The afternoon **sell-off ran buy-side then sold off** — but the **model gave NO setup** during it. "Algorithmic principles simply don't appear in markets unlikely to deliver." The **valid** setup was the **bullish leg** toward 12553.25. Don't force a trade onto every liquidity run.

---

## PM Session Principle

> When the market **consolidates through lunch**, find the **lunch lows**, wait for them to be **run**, and expect the move to **resume** (toward the true draw). *(Same lesson as [Episode 9](../ep09-power-of-3-ny-pm/README.md).)*
