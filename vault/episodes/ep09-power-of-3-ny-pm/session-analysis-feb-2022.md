## Monday, February 14, 2022

### Daily Context
- Price was in a [discount zone](../../concepts/market-structure.md) (below 50% Fibonacci retracement of the daily range)
- The weekly open was bearish, with Monday dropping into sell-side liquidity

### What Happened (Chronological)

#### 1. Sell-Side Liquidity Sweep
- Price dropped below **short-term lows**, taking out [sell-side liquidity](../../concepts/liquidity.md)
- Retail stops triggered below these lows
- This completed the **manipulation phase** of [Power of 3](../../concepts/power-of-3.md)

#### 2. Retracement Toward 50% Fib
- After sweeping SSL, price retraced toward the 50% Fibonacci retracement
- Monday's close featured an **indecisive candle** — signaling uncertainty but implying a possible retracement

#### 3. Early NY Session (9:30 AM)
- Price opened near the lows
- Dipped further into a **bullish [order block](../../concepts/order-blocks.md)** (cluster of down-close candles on 5-min)
- The order block indicated institutional buy interest

#### 4. Rally from the Order Block
- Price rallied strongly after touching the bullish OB zone
- The market repeatedly tested the imbalance zone ([fair value gap](../../concepts/fair-value-gaps.md)) on both daily and 5-minute charts
- These FVG levels confirmed as key support

#### 5. PM Session Entry
- Optimal entry identified near the order block in the afternoon session
- Price had retreated into the [liquidity](../../concepts/liquidity.md) zone before the continuation higher
- Entry: Near/inside the bullish order block after the retreat
- Stop: Below the OB low (~14.25 points risk)
- Target: Toward buy-side liquidity above (~3.5:1 R:R)

### Trade Parameters (Monday)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Direction | Long |
| Entry zone | Bullish order block (5-min) |
| Stop loss | Below OB low (~14.25 handles) |
| Contracts | 6 micro NQ |
| Dollar risk | ~$85 |
| Target reward | ~$300 |
| R:R ratio | ~3.5:1 |

## Tuesday, February 15, 2022

### Daily Context
- Monday's indecisive candle left the market in limbo
- Overnight/London session produced a **strong run** — aggressive price movement during pre-market

### What Happened (Chronological)

#### 1. Strong Overnight Run
- Price ran aggressively during London/NASDAQ pre-open sessions
- By the time NY opened, much of the move was already done
- This triggered the "avoid" rule: do not trade NY after strong overnight runs

#### 2. Morning Consolidation
- Price consolidated following the big overnight move
- Rather than trending further, it chopped sideways
- This is typical after exhaustion of overnight momentum

#### 3. Pseudo Judas Swing
- Early morning price action created a **pseudo Judas swing**
- **Relative equal lows** formed — two or more lows at nearly the same price
- These equal lows built [sell-side liquidity](../../concepts/liquidity.md) below

#### 4. Liquidity Trap Setup
- The relative equal lows were visible to retail traders
- Stop-losses clustered just below these levels
- The presenter identified this as a **probable stop-loss hunt**

> "Liquidity builds below these relative equal lows, setting the stage for a retail trap where stops are taken out before rallying."

#### 5. Recommended Approach
- The presenter advised **avoiding** the NY AM session entirely
- Recommended waiting until **after 1:00 PM ET** (New York lunch)
- Only after lunch-hour lows are taken should a trader look for entries
- See [New York PM Session](new-york-pm-session.md) for this strategy

### Key Lesson from Tuesday
- **Patience over action** — Not every session is tradeable
- Strong overnight moves consume the day's range
- Wait for fresh liquidity to build (during lunch) before entering
- The PM session after consolidation provides cleaner setups

## Lessons from This Session

1. **Monday offered a textbook setup** — All elements aligned (discount + SSL sweep + OB + FVG + PM timing)
2. **Tuesday required discipline** — Recognizing when NOT to trade is as important as entries
3. **Overnight runs change the playbook** — Big moves pre-market reduce NY session opportunity
4. **Patience pays** — Waiting for the PM session with fresh liquidity produces better trades
5. **Algorithmic behavior is consistent** — The patterns repeat; the timing varies
