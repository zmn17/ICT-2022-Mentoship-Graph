## Session Overview

The New York PM session (approximately 1:00 PM – 4:00 PM ET) is where many of the highest-probability ICT setups complete. It represents the **distribution** phase of [Power of 3](../../concepts/power-of-3.md) on an intraday basis — after morning manipulation has occurred and liquidity has been hunted.

---

## Daily Session Breakdown

| Time (ET) | Session | Role in Power of 3 |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| 8:00 PM – 5:00 AM | Asia/Overnight | Accumulation |
| 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM | London | Pre-manipulation / Range building |
| 5:00 AM – 8:30 AM | London/Pre-Market | Overnight runs, liquidity building |
| 9:30 AM | NY Open | Initial manipulation (Judas swing) |
| 9:30 AM – 12:00 PM | NY AM | Manipulation / False moves |
| 12:00 PM – 1:00 PM | NY Lunch | Consolidation / New liquidity builds |
| **1:00 PM – 4:00 PM** | **NY PM** | **Distribution / True move** |

---

## Why the NY PM Session is Important

From Episode 9:

1. **Morning manipulation completes** — By 1:00 PM, the morning's liquidity hunts are done
2. **Lunch-hour liquidity is built** — The sideways chop during lunch creates new stop-loss clusters
3. **The true move unfolds** — After lunch lows are taken, the real directional move begins
4. **Cleaner price action** — Less choppy than the morning session

---

## When to AVOID the NY PM Session

### After Strong Overnight Runs
> "Strong overnight runs during London/NASDAQ pre-open sessions commonly require a wait-for-consolidation strategy rather than chasing momentum."

If price has already moved 100+ points overnight:
- Do NOT chase the move at NY open
- Wait for price to **consolidate** after the big move
- The consolidation may last through the AM session
- Only look for setups **after 1:00 PM** when new liquidity has formed

### During Choppy/Sideways Price Action
> "When price action is sideways and erratic, it's better to scale back trade frequency to avoid losses in low-probability conditions."

If the NY AM session shows no clear direction or structure, stand aside.

## NY PM Session Trading Rules (From Episode 9)

### Rule 1: Wait for NY Lunch Lows to Be Taken
- Lunch hour (12:00–1:00 PM) creates a consolidation range
- [Sell-side liquidity](../../concepts/liquidity.md) builds below the lunch lows
- After 1:00 PM, watch for a sweep of these lunch lows
- This sweep is your signal that manipulation is complete

### Rule 2: Identify the Order Block After the Sweep
- Once lunch lows are taken, look for an [order block](../../concepts/order-blocks.md) that holds
- Enter near/inside the OB after the liquidity sweep
- The move should be toward the opposite (buy-side) liquidity

### Rule 3: Use 5-Minute Timeframe for Entries
- The 5-minute chart provides the clearest intraday [fair value gaps](../../concepts/fair-value-gaps.md) and order blocks
- Zoom in after identifying the daily bias and lunch sweep

### Rule 4: Don't Chase After 3:00 PM
- Most of the distribution move occurs between 1:00–3:00 PM
- After 3:00 PM, moves become less reliable and spreads can widen

## Optimal NY PM Setup (Step by Step)

1. **Morning context:** Note overnight runs and morning [liquidity](../../concepts/liquidity.md) hunts
2. **Lunch consolidation:** Observe range built during 12:00–1:00 PM
3. **Post-lunch sweep:** Wait for price to take out lunch lows (sell-side liquidity)
4. **Order block entry:** Enter long at the [bullish order block](../../concepts/order-blocks.md) formed after the sweep
5. **Target:** Buy-side liquidity above (morning high or daily level)
6. **Stop:** Below the order block low — see [Risk Management](../../concepts/risk-management.md)

## The 9:30 AM NY Open (Context)

While the focus is on PM session, the 9:30 AM open provides context:
- Price at 9:30 often gaps or displaces from the overnight range
- [Fair value gaps](../../concepts/fair-value-gaps.md) created at the open are later filled
- The open establishes the **initial manipulation** that PM trading exploits

## The "Pseudo Judas Swing" (Feb 15 Example)

- Sometimes the overnight move creates a false direction (Judas swing)
- Relative equal lows form during the morning session
- These are then hunted in the PM session
- The true move follows after this PM manipulation
