## What This Teaches

A New York index day is a **split day** — a **morning session** (8:30–noon) and a **PM session** (after the noon–1:00 lunch) — and **each half contains multiple repeatable setups**. Using Power of 3 and premium/discount, ICT trades a **bearish morning** into a daily bullish order block, then a **bullish PM** that engineers buy-side liquidity to reprice discount → premium. Introduces the **mitigation block** and **body-anchored Fibonacci** targeting.

## Why "Multiple Setups" Matters

> Missing one entry is fine — **the concepts repeat**. The morning alone offers 2–3 setups (volatility at the 9:30 open); the afternoon offers its own. The same logic (displacement → FVG → retrace → liquidity target) fractally recurs on the 15-, 5-, and 1-minute charts.

## The Core Insight

> A New York day is **two sessions**, each full of repeating setups. Frame it with **Power of 3** and **premium/discount**: the morning distributes **down** toward the daily bullish **order block** (the draw), the afternoon **engineers buy-side liquidity** to reprice back **up**. Same fractal logic — displacement, FVG, retrace, liquidity — over and over.
