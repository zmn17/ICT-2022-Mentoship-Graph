## What This Teaches

How the **dollar's regime** — **risk-on vs risk-off** — drives every correlated asset. A **rising dollar** = **risk-off** (flight to quality): capital floods into the dollar and out of risk assets, so FX and stock indices fall. ICT reviews his published call (DXY down to **99.92** then rally; ES weak into relative equal lows amid seasonal **May** weakness) and shows the lone clean **London-open** short built from displacement + FVG + MSS — read as *extremely* bearish because price couldn't even rally above the key opening prices.

## The Core Insight

> The **dollar sets the regime**. A rising dollar is **risk-off** — a flight to quality that drags FX and indices **down**. Read the dollar first, let it set your **bias**, then execute the intraday model. When price **can't even rally above** the key opening prices, the market is telling you it's **extremely heavy** — take the clean London-open **displacement + FVG + MSS** short.
