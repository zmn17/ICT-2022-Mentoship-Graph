## What This Teaches

Trading **forex in the London session** through **intermarket relationships**. ICT reads the **Dollar Index** as a barometer: it's engineered down to **99.92** (a buy level), which — because DXY and EUR are **inverse** — sets EUR up to rally into a **false breakout** above the prior week's high during the **London Kill Zone (2–5 AM NY)**. That false push is the **Judas swing**; the short is executed off a 2-minute FVG with **sub-5-pip risk** and **fixed-percentage** money management.


## The Entry Model (Step-by-Step)

| Step | Action | Concept |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | **Market-maker view:** the DXY is engineered down to **99.92** (a 3-pip zone, 99.95→99.92) as a buy level. | [Intermarket Correlation](../../concepts/intermarket-correlation.md) |
| 2 | **Inverse barometer:** DXY down → EUR up. When the dollar hits 99.92, EUR should rally. | [Intermarket Correlation](../../concepts/intermarket-correlation.md) |
| 3 | Both extremes form in the **London Kill Zone (2–5 AM NY)** — DXY low / EUR high of day. | [Trading Sessions](../../concepts/trading-sessions.md) |
| 4 | EUR runs **above the previous week's high** at the *exact instant* DXY tags 99.92 → **false breakout**. | [Liquidity](../../concepts/liquidity.md) |
| 5 | That false push = a **Judas swing** (sweeps liquidity above the old high, then reverses). | [Judas Swing](../../concepts/judas-swing.md) |
| 6 | On the **2-min** EUR chart: **displacement** lower breaks a short-term low → **market structure shift** + **FVG**. | [Displacement](../../concepts/displacement.md) / [Market Structure Shift](../../concepts/market-structure-shift.md) |
| 7 | **Entry:** limit in the FVG *after* price energetically moves away; **stop** just above the swing high (**<5 pips**). | [Optimal Trade Entry](../../concepts/optimal-trade-entry.md) |
| 8 | **Target:** Fib from the most energetic low → **50%** first target; deeper imbalance for a full-day hold. | [Fibonacci Targeting](../../concepts/fibonacci-targeting.md) |

## Money Management

> Think in **fixed percentage**, not dollars: **1% maximum**, preferably **½% or ¼%**. With a **sub-5-pip** stop, position size is set by the percentage risk — not a dollar feeling.

See [Risk Management](../../concepts/risk-management.md).

## The Barometer Insight

```
   DXY ─────╲                       EUR ────────╱ false breakout above
             ╲ 99.92 buy level                 ╱  prev week high (Judas swing)
              ▼ (London Kill Zone low)        ╱
   at the SAME instant → confirms EUR's high is FALSE → short EUR
```

You can only read EUR's breakout as false by **relating it to the dollar** (the measuring instrument). See [Intermarket Correlation](../../concepts/intermarket-correlation.md).

## The Core Insight

> In forex, **relate your market to a barometer**. The dollar engineered down to **99.92** (inverse to EUR) exposes EUR's push above the prior week's high as a **false breakout / Judas swing** in the **London Kill Zone**. Short the 2-min **FVG** after displacement, risk **<5 pips** at a **fixed %**, and target the **50%** Fib.
