## What This Teaches

Two complementary tools on one ES day: a **bullish continuation into a daily bearish order block**, confirmed by an **SMT divergence** at the 8:30/9:30 windows. NASDAQ made a **lower low** while the S&P held a **higher low** → **accumulation** of longs (not real weakness). Price sprang out of a **fair value gap** toward the order block's **mean threshold** (with 4,000 as an objective).

## SMT Timing Rule

> Look for **SMT divergence around 9:30** *after* an early run **before or at 7:00**. The divergence at the news windows (8:30/9:30) confirms whether the extreme is a **stop run** (accumulation/distribution) or genuine.

See [SMT Divergence](../../concepts/smt-divergence.md).

## The Core Insight

> **Order block + SMT divergence** stack into one high-confidence read. A daily **bearish order block** is the upside objective (reaching its **mean threshold** favors continuation); the **SMT divergence** — **NQ lower low vs ES higher low** — reveals **accumulation**, not weakness. Buy the discount **FVG/OTE** and let price **spring** toward the order block.
