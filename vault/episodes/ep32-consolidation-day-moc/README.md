## What This Teaches

How to **recognize a consolidation day** and where its **one clean trade** lives. After an **outside-down-close** day, price traded toward the prior daily low but **fell one handle short (3856 vs 3855)** → downside continuation died, a deceptive **double-bottom** lured traders, and odds reverted to **50/50** as price rotated to the middle. The real algorithmic trade was the **Market-On-Close reversal (3–4 PM)**: run buy-side above equal highs, then reverse back to the **daily midpoint**.

```

```

## The Selectivity Lesson

> On a **consolidation day**, "nobody is allowed to make money" in the choppy middle — odds are **50/50**. Recognize the day type, **stand aside** through the chop, and only engage the **MOC close-reversal** — a run to buy-side that reverses back to the daily midpoint, punishing both breakout longs and stopped-out shorts.

---

## Quick-Reference Checklist

```
□ Prior day = outside-down-close → expect consolidation
□ Price falls SHORT of the target old low → continuation dies (double-bottom lure)
□ Back to middle of range = 50/50 → stand aside through chop
□ Vertical lines at 3:00 & 4:00 PM NY (MOC window)
□ Pre-close run above relative equal highs (buy-side grab)
□ MOC reversal: sell into liquidity → drive to daily midpoint
□ 1-min: run → swing high + FVG → retrace → midpoint
```
